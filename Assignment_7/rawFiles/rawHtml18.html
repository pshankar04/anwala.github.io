By Kerry O'Connor - April 30, 2018
There divides me from the dead 
A wall of difficult dreams 
Federico Garcia Lorca




Death hawked me
on the first Monday in May
with a twinge of pain
that grubbed its way up the back of my leg
the larvae of a graveyard moth
incubated in tissue
now uncocooning from its nest of nerves
worming trails of poison
to mix with blood.

There is grace in the agony
that skulls its unwizening wings
and proboscises sweet lymph with uncurled tongue
spectre owling through darkness
while the life of its host
dwindles to the brink – blink – blink –
Still it shadows the edge of my dreams
chews me from sleep
to feast once again on the flesh it has maimed.

The Cuckoo, A Nursery Rhyme



Ah, love, let me lie here in the tangled aftermath
of bedsheets, uncovered, aglow in the light of one small lamp
and watch you play your guitar through sleepy eyes.

‘Do you remember this one?’ you ask me and I do
but I cannot find the words of the song while memorizing your face,
your fingers gliding, plucking the strings, your lips humming.

By Kerry O'Connor - April 27, 2018
Not only under ground are the brains of men 
Eaten by maggots. 
Life in itself 
Is nothing
Edna St Vincent Millay




A year ago, the ground cracked wide
beneath my feet –
darkness clutched my ankle
and I fell into my grave.

It is a lonely thing to live interred –
the smell of cold clay
sifts into your skin, an embrace
whispering of decomposition.

The sky is reduced to rectangle
devoid of the sun –
the rain finds your place in the night
and seeps through veins to heart.

Imperceptibly, your brain grows roots
fine as hairs, thick as fingers –
when it is time to rise, you find
you have grown attached to death.

A year ago, I went down to earth
but I did not die –
I returned to the land peeling off
tattered remains, a ghost of myself.




For Izy's Out of Standard prompt on day 26 of poetry writing month.

By Kerry O'Connor - April 24, 2018
Appendix

“Are you prepared to die? If that’s O.K., then you’re a candidate for going.” 
 Elon Musk


Mars, 2028 CE
You contemplate the view
from the porthole of the Heart of Gold –
the alien landscape bears its own battle scars,
impact craters of heavy bombardment
a billion years ago.
You wonder if dust is worth the fight –
What human sacrifice will be made
on the altar of this temple,
this bloody planet
in the name of the God of War?




The initial goal of SpaceX’s Mars mission will be to establish a self-sufficient colony. Elon Musk, who has been vocal about his desire to live and eventually die on Mars, said that to pick the first colonizers, the qualifications for going would include a high risk tolerance for mortality. By Kerry O'Connor - April 23, 2018
The lunatic, the lover and the poet
Are of imagination all compact.
William Shakespeare




There is an art to eating a pomegranate.

First, crack open the shell
or slice it down the middle to reveal
the gleaming brain
cradled in creamy pith, stained now
with carmine drops
blood-like.

Do not try to pick each seed from its case,
rather tear your half open
and bite
fill your mouth and squeeze the juice
between palate and tongue
let lips be stained.

This fruitful madness is yours to consume.



For An Antic Disposition on Day 23 of poetry writing month.
