Question 2- How effective is the combination of your main product and ancillary texts?



Images in better quality:



 





Together, the music video, music advert and digipak come together to create one promotional piece. The music video is a way of illustrating and making their work visual for their audience and so that those watching can distinguish their genre and style. Similarly, the digipak also represents the artist and depicts and promotes their style, the main lead in the band, their genre and look. It also so that the audience can have a first taste of the artist(s) and their work or as a collector' item, all while the audience feel as if they are receiving something more from the artist/band and it also acts as a form of merchandise; which in turn will bring in profit for the artist/band. Lastly, the advert promotes their material to their audience and is a way of catching the audience' attention to bring in profit and consider the product and is a way to target the audience through traditional means of print. Using synergy, all three texts relate and link in some way which makes the audience able to distinguish a theme and a connection between the three which promotes them further.


The evident synergy in our promotional package is the colour scheme, theme and font.


On both ancillaries we used the same muted and pastel colours to distinguish the genre. Using the same effect shows a link between them and helps to show the style and look of the artist.
 
The   hand-written font is used in each product which connotes the idea of being casual and fun due to the style, which is the idea we wanted to create regarding our audience so that the audience would get a creative atmosphere that from our artist through the products.


The occurring theme of the beach is seen throughout everything as a whole. This means that they link through synergy. This is the most key aspect we wanted to promote throughout our piece. We chose to really emphasise this as throughout  the song the lyrics mention aspects of the beach e.g. the tide, sea which is a metaphor for her feelings and emotions towards the guy. With this being the strongest emotion and theme in the song
we really wanted to emphasise it and did so by having aspects of the beach in each product and did this by using footage and images of the beach.


Exhibition


When it comes to media there is 'traditional' and 'new' media. 'Traditional' media regards forms of advertising  such as television, print and radio. 'New' media means  mass communication using digital technologies such the internet, YouTube, Facebook, Vimeo and many more.


Our music video is exhibited on YouTube and Facebook where it is available for all to see and find. The benefits of using this new form of media is that they are free to upload our videos onto, it is quicker and easier to reach and find, it is more interactive and convergence. The cons of this is that it can be unreliable, for example if you lose internet connection you won't be able to access it, and that individuals are able to go anonymous and be negative about your posted products by commenting hate whereas this wouldn't happen with a poster or CD in a shop. We would also create a Facebook page specifically for the artist to help promote the work. This is simple and free to do through Facebook and also allows us to use convergence as it means we can offer downloads to our audience and free listening of Mp3 clips.


 Using traditional methods, I would also put my music video onto the music channel MTV. This is due to MTV being one of the most infamous music channels since the late 80's and having the ideal age range we want to target. Additionally, it also has multiple music events occurring one of which is the 'MTV Indie Awkwards', which fits our genre perfectly and could potentially attract our audience. The pros of this are that it is  easy for the to find and watch and that music channels often repeat what they have already played which means our song would potentially get listened to on a loop. The cons are that the audience can't skip songs they dislike and may change the channel which could result in them missing our song.








Our music advert would be exhibited in an indie magazine, such as NME. This is due to their target audience consisting of a large amount of student readers who are our ideal target age. The pros of this traditional form of media are that they can target mass audiences, which is apparent from the statistics NME have that show a massive 388,000 in their readership. Another pro is that you can target your advert accurately and appropriately, for example I wouldn't necessarily advertise our artist in Kerrang! due to this magazine promoting more punk and rock genres. However, cons of adverts can be that you have to pay a fee to have it made and put into a magazine and that it may not be effective or capture the reader' attention and become easily bypassed which can be a waste of time and money. I would also consider having my advert displayed on social networking sites (such as Facebook) using the user' personal information to target them accurately and meet my audience criteria through viral marketing.




Our digipak would be sold traditionally in stores like HMV in the form of a CD and as new media on websites such as iTunes. I chose to combine the two so that it appeals to both the older generation who may like a hardcopy and the new generation who are more technological. I chose HMV as they are one of the most popular shops for selling items such as CD's and DVD's. I chose to sell the digipak online on music stores due to the increasing rise in the use of technology and how hardcopies such as CDs are slowly fading out. Having it available on sites such as iTunes also allows our products to have convergence as the user will be online, potentially on their phone going onto the store to download and purchase content all while being and doing multiple technical tasks on one device. This also allows us to distribute it more easily and more cheaply due to not having to make a product case and print it. ﻿







We also used cross-media convergence in the form of  the software we used to make our products. We used Photoshop to create our advert and digipak but also used the website Dafont to get the text we wanted to put on our products, showing they worked together to make our final ancillaries.

Q3- What have you learned from your audience feedback?



Audience


Our audience are aged around 16 to 30,both genders and who enjoy energetic and indie music. The psychographics  category we're targeting is explorers. This group are drawn to energy and individualism, which are what the band are described as. This group also tend to consist of a younger age group/ students, which the band members are themselves which allows demographics to relate to their audience more.




Preliminary




The responses we received from our preliminary to final video were all positive and the viewers could see improvement in varying areas. The areas  they felt we most improved on were:


  • The cutting to the beat and doing it in an effective way that made our piece fun and creative, especially as our song had many opportunities to cut to the beat.
  • It looked more conventional, which I believe was mainly due to the change in genre as our preliminary genre was folk/alt and the artist was quite eccentric in his videos. Ours was of the indie genre which is very popular and is a lot more conventional to follow.
  • The overall style and appearance looked nicer, alongside the effect we added and used which we didn't get a chance to play around with in the prelim as the video didn't have any effects to add.
  • The shots were more varied and conventional. This improved more as in the preliminary there was only the typical long-shot/mid-shot and extreme long-shot repeatedly, however in our final video we got the opportunity to experiment with close-ups, extreme-close-ups and mid-shots, long-shots.
  • We also changed the typography so that it had a more 'indie' style to fit the genre.



Research






The above vox pop was us researching our audience and their feedback on music videos and music genres.


This helped us to discover more about her target audience and their favourite song, what like they liked in a video, the style and more. This helped us to conclude our ideas further and what we should include.


From this we learnt that the participants more favoured and common mentioned genre was indie. This was an advantage for us as we had decided to do the indie genre and this strengthened our decision to remain with our initial chosen genre. We also asked what their favourite music videos were, most of which were of the indie genre which strengthen the idea of choosing an indie song. This also helped us to see which style narratives our audience liked and preferred and if they had a strong storyline. The below were the mentioned music videos:

We asked if they preferred performance or narrative and to what extent in a music video. The common response was a mix of the two to keep it interesting and varied. This impacted our ideas as to what to include and how to structure our piece so that it appealed to viewers and didn't become boring or confusing. Also, we asked which narrative style they preferred, the most popular answer was amplification so that people can interpret their own ideas around the video and so that the narrative isn't instantly revealed and given away too quickly. This impacted us in the way we were going to include a narrative so that the audience could think for themselves but not have it given away at once, hence why amplification was the likely way to structure our video.


One question we asked was what people liked to see in a video and what appealed to them. We received the following comments:


  • Different instruments
  • Storyline/narrative
  • Band performance
  • Effects
  • Montage style
  • Having fun
  • Different locations


This led us to thinking which points we could easily address and incorporate into our and which we felt could fit well. For example, what locations would fit with the song but would be varied and how could a montage be incorporated.


Lastly, we asked where our audience were most likely to watch music videos. Over half of those we asked said YouTube which is ideal as that is where we uploaded our final product and any draft attempts, meaning people are more likely to watch them or find them.

Firstly, we conducted a focus group to gather our target audience's opinion and ideas when initially hearing the song. A focus group is a group of people brought together to contribute in a discussion about a product or idea before it has become real/is made.

What theme(s) emerged when listening to the track? 








 Overall, candidates felt the same themes we did which were love and happiness, which is what we wanted to portray in our video and through our narrative choice. Additionally, they also felt it was upbeat and bubbly which is the vibe we wanted to go for in our music choice.






What would you expect to see in the video?




Overall, the participants adhered to the ideas we had ourselves. What characters would you picture?.
